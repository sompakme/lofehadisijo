Richard S. Trethewey (born c. 1956 in Dedham, Massachusetts) is an American plumber and HVAC contractor best known for his appearances on the television shows This Old House and its spin-offs, Ask This Old House and Inside This Old House. Trethewey Brothers lent their skills to producer Russell Morash’s then-new home renovation television series in 1979. Richard’s father, Ron, held the position for a season before passing it on to his son, who has been in charge ever since. In the  Commonwealth of Massachusetts, Richard is a licenced journeyman and master plumber. He supervises all plumbing, heating, and air conditioning improvements for each This Old House project. He provides his advice to viewers who write in with concerns about plumbing and HVAC for Ask This Old House, and he sometimes visits a viewer’s house. He left Trethewey Brothers in 1990 to start his own firm, RST, Inc. RST represents various lines of plumbing and HVAC equipment, ranging from pipes to full systems, as a manufacturer’s agent. The business, which is based in Westwood, Massachusetts, employs nine employees and covers most of New England. Richard Trethewey’s kids, who are also in his father’s company, have recently made appearances on This Old House.
