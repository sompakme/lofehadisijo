Super Mario Bros is about to surpass Frozen II as the highest-grossing animated film

October 01, 2024
blog

Contrary to previous years, we are nearly in the moddle of the year and only one movie has crossed the billion-dollar barrier. Super Mario Bros is about to reach $1.3 billion at the worldwide box office, the animated film is a behemoth against any competitor this year. But how does it fare against some of the highest-grossing animated movies of all time? Looking at the list, the sequel to Frozen sits in first place with $1.45 billion and the first Frozen movie moved down to third with $1.28 billion. Should the Mario Brosfilm remain in theatres for at least a few more weeks, they will definitely have a chance at defeating Frozen II. If this happens, this would be a significant win for Universal against Disney. But what the Super Mario Bros film has done truly defies even the most unthinkable odds.

Super Mario Bros is a videogame-based project done right

For many years, making a great series or movie that was based in a videogame was considered impossible. Many tried doing their best and failed at least in one category to consider. Whether it was the story, the medium, or even the production of the film. There was always something that made it lack a certain something that made it a success. Super Mario is the very first videogame-based film that becomes part of the billion dollar club. But what did they do right? In the past, other films such as the Sonic franchise, Tomb Raider, or Pokemon tried their shot at doing it live action. But for some reason, there wasn't anybody who actually tried doing it in animation.

People who play videogames want to feel that nostalgia aspect that triggers their memories and live action does a poor job at that. Even if the Super Mario Bros story is not great, that nostalgia factor does play a huge part in the film's success. On a side note, the actual most successful animated movie of all time is the "live action" The Lion King but that one is not considered animated because they still have Mowgli starring in it in live action form. With this level of success, Universal Studios has a great chance to create their own Nintendo Universe by exploring other meorable stories of the videogame company. The question is: Will they be animated or live action? Elsa, Olaf, and company had a good run. It was fun while it lasted but there's a new animated king in town. It'sa him, Mario!
