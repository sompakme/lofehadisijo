Plank Position: Which Plank Position is most effective?

July 03, 2024
blog

Planks are one of the exercises which are very common in many athletes' workout routines. They are regarded as the groundwork for various bodyweight drills, as they help you strengthen several parts of your body.

The standard straight-arm plank and the forearm plank are considered as the most popular plank exercises, but you might be wondering which one is more effective.

How to perform the straight-arm plank

Get into a push-up position with your toes and hands planted into the floor. Your body should be forming a straight line and you should hold this position for the desired time and keep your neck in a neutral position while performing the exercise.

How to perform the forearm plank

This is the most common plank drill. Get into a traditional push-up position, bend your elbows and plant your forearms into the floor. Once you are in the forearm plank position, make sure that your body is forming a straight line from your feet to your head. Hold this movement for the desired time, but if you can't do that, just try to perform it in intervals of 10 seconds.

Which plank position is most effective?

Both planks are beneficial for your body as they help you strengthen several areas by working various muscles. The straight-arm plank engages the triceps and the core, being the most preferred plank drill for those who are willing to strengthen their upper body.

Meanwhile, the forearm plank helps you strengthen the transverse abdominis muscle and works better for people who target their abs.
