 Early Years of Jon Rahm

Jon Rahm, the famous golfer from Spain, was born in 1994 in Barrika, Spain. From a very young age, Jon showed a special talent for sports, and one sport in particular caught his attention – golf.

 Family Support and Inspiration

Jon Rahm’s parents, Edorta Rahm and Ángela Rodríguez, played a big part in nurturing his interest in sports. They were inspired by the 1997 Ryder Cup in Valderrama, which motivated Jon to explore the world of golf. Thanks to his parents’ encouragement, golf soon became his passion.

A Golfer’s Dedication

Jon Rahm worked very hard to make his golf dreams come true. This dedication led him to become one of the world’s top golfers, with accomplishments like winning the 2021 U.S. Open and representing Spain internationally.

Early Golfing Days

Jon Rahm’s golf journey took off at the Centro Nacional de Golf in Madrid, Spain. Even though he tried his hand at other sports like soccer and canoeing, he decided to focus solely on golf when he was just 14 years old.

School Success and Scholarship

Rahm attended IES Ortega y Gassett high school in Madrid, where he won various European golf competitions. He even received a scholarship to play golf at Arizona State University in the United States. This opportunity allowed him to grow as a golfer and compete at a high level.

 Jon Rahm’s Roots

Early Coach and Practice
