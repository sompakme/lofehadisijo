Brooke Williamson Career- Restauranteur

At the age of six despite watching cartoons show Brooke was fond of watching Cookery shows and then decided to become a Chef in her life. Her career journey started at the age of 17. She first worked as a teacher assistant at the famous Epicurean Institute of Los Angeles. Then she became a Pastry assistant at Fenix in the Argyle Hotel.

Her dedication and cooking quality supported Brooke Williamson to get an opportunity of working with Chef Michael McCartys’ at his restaurant Michael’s of Santa Monica. She served here as the youngest sous chef. Willing to learn more and get a better life Brooke then started working with Daniel’s restaurant in New York City.

Two years after this she served in the highest position of her career as an executive chef at the notable restaurant in LA named Boxer. She then stepped forward in her career and opened different restaurants collaborating with her husband Nick Roberts.

Not only this Brooke Williamson also won Bravo TV’s popular culinary show Top Chef season 14 in 2017. She also participated in Top Chef Duels, Knife Fight, House of Food, and Guy’s Grocery Games. She remained a judge of the BBQ Brawl show and also won the Knife Fight reality show.
