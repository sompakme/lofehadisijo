Rodman Edward “Rod” Serling (December 25, 1924 – June 28, 1975) was an American screenwriter, dramatist, television producer, and narrator best known for his 1950s live television dramas and science fiction anthology TV series The Twilight Zone. On and off the screen, Serling was involved in politics and worked to shape television industry standards. He was regarded as Hollywood’s “angry young man,” fighting with television executives and advertisers on a variety of topics such as censorship, racism, and war.
