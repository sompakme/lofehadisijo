Post-UTME stands for Post Unified Tertiary Matriculation Exam. Post-UTMEs were first introduced in Nigeria in 2005. Universities, polytechnics, and colleges normally hold post-UTME exams or screenings to help in the selection process for candidates who meet the required JAMB cut-off mark. Discover the universities that will write post-UTME in 2024/2025.

Universities whose post-UTME form is out

The Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) recently announced 19 July 2024 as the commencement date for the sale of the 2024/2025 post-UTME forms across all universities in Nigeria.

All schools requiring prospective students to sit their post-UTMEs will start selling their post-UTME screening forms then. Candidates are advised to start preparing for the exams right away. The post-UTME closing date for all schools is yet to be officially announced.

Post-UTME registration

Many universities conduct their post-UTMEs online. Each institution has its own system. Most have post-UTME registration portals, where candidates should register before sitting their exams.

It is important to note that these examinations may be different in different institutions. Remember, each university is at liberty to screen its candidates as they deem fit.

What is the use of post-UTME?

Post-UTME is used to test students' knowledge and preparedness for university education. It covers multiple subjects, including English, Mathematics, and General Knowledge.

What is the full meaning of post-UTME?

Post-UTME stands for Post Unified Tertiary Matriculation Exam. It is a screening used to help in the selection process for candidates who meet the required JAMB cut-off mark.

What subject is written for post-UTME?

UTME subjects include English, Mathematics, and Physics. The papers can also include Biology, Chemistry, Computer Science, Computer Studies, and Economics, depending on the course you wish to pursue.

How do you check your post-UTME status?

After sitting your exams, you can confirm your status on the post-UTME portal.
