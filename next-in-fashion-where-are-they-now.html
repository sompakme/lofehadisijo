Next In Fashion Where Are They Now?

August 10, 2024
blog

The fashion industry is ever-evolving, and so are the careers of those who participate in fashion design competitions. Netflix’s “Next In Fashion” is a prime example of a platform that has showcased the talents of numerous designers, all vying for a chance to make their mark in the fashion world. Since the show’s conclusion, many viewers have been curious about the whereabouts and progress of these talented individuals. This article delves into the lives and careers of the “Next In Fashion” contestants, exploring where they are now and how the show has impacted their professional journeys.

Introduction to “Next In Fashion”

“Next In Fashion” is a Netflix reality show that premiered in January 2020. Hosted by fashion icons Tan France and Alexa Chung, the series featured 18 designers from around the world competing for a $250,000 prize and the opportunity to debut their collection with luxury fashion retailer Net-a-Porter. The show was well-received for its creative challenges, diverse cast, and insight into the fashion design process.

Winner’s Update: Minju Kim

  • Since winning “Next In Fashion,” Minju Kim has continued to develop her eponymous brand, MINJUKIM.
  • Her designs have been featured in various international fashion weeks and have been worn by celebrities.
  • Kim’s brand has seen significant growth and continues to embrace her signature playful and artistic style.

Runner-Up: Daniel Fletcher

  • Post-show, Daniel Fletcher has been appointed as the menswear artistic director at Fiorucci.
  • He has also continued to work on his own label, DANIEL w. FLETCHER, which has been gaining traction in the fashion industry.
  • Fletcher’s designs have been spotted on various celebrities and have been featured in high-profile editorials.

Contestant Updates

Each contestant from “Next In Fashion” has taken a unique path since their time on the show. Here’s a look at what some of them have been up to:

Angel Chen

  • Angel Chen has expanded her brand globally and has collaborated with various international brands.
  • Her work has continued to blend Eastern and Western aesthetics, gaining a strong following.

Charles Lu

  • Charles Lu has returned to his bridalwear roots, creating stunning gowns for private clients.
  • He has also been involved in several fashion shows and events.

Ashton Hirota

  • Ashton Hirota’s brand, ASHTON MICHAEL, has been focused on creating custom pieces for celebrities and musicians.
  • He has also been an advocate for sustainable fashion practices.

Kiki Kitty

  • Kiki Kitty has continued to work on her brand, KIKI KITTY, focusing on empowering women through fashion.
  • She has also been active in mentoring young designers.

Carli Pearson

  • Carli Pearson’s brand, CIMONE, has been pushing boundaries with innovative designs and has been featured in several fashion publications.

Marco Morante

  • Marco Morante’s brand, Marco Marco Underwear, has continued to create bold and colorful designs for the LGBTQ+ community.

Impact of “Next In Fashion” on Contestants’ Careers

The exposure from “Next In Fashion” has undoubtedly had an impact on the contestants’ careers. Many have seen increased media attention, collaborations, and opportunities to showcase their work on global platforms. The show has also helped some designers secure investment and support to grow their brands.

Challenges Faced Post-Show

Despite the benefits, some contestants have faced challenges, such as navigating the fashion industry during the COVID-19 pandemic, which has affected supply chains, production, and sales. Adapting to the digital shift in fashion retail and marketing has also been a hurdle for some.

Continued Education and Advocacy

Several “Next In Fashion” alumni have engaged in further education, either by attending prestigious fashion schools or by offering workshops and talks. They have also been involved in advocacy work, promoting sustainability, diversity, and inclusion within the fashion industry.

Collaborations and Brand Expansions

Collaborations with other brands and designers have been a significant step for many “Next In Fashion” contestants. These partnerships have allowed them to reach new audiences and explore different aspects of design and production.

Media Features and Celebrity Endorsements

Media features in fashion magazines, online publications, and social media have helped elevate the profiles of the contestants. Celebrity endorsements have also played a role in boosting their brand recognition and credibility.

Future Prospects

The future looks bright for many of the “Next In Fashion” contestants. With a solid foundation laid by the show, they are poised to continue making waves in the fashion industry, whether through expanding their brands, engaging in new collaborations, or continuing to innovate within their niches.

FAQ Section

Has “Next In Fashion” been renewed for another season?

As of the knowledge cutoff date, “Next In Fashion” has not been renewed for a second season by Netflix.

Can I purchase designs from “Next In Fashion” contestants?

Yes, many of the contestants have their own brands and sell their designs online or through various retailers.

What impact did the show have on the winner’s career?

The show provided the winner, Minju Kim, with significant exposure, a cash prize, and the opportunity to launch a collection with Net-a-Porter, all of which have contributed to the growth and success of her brand.

Conclusion

“Next In Fashion” served as a launching pad for many talented designers, providing them with exposure and opportunities that have propelled their careers forward. From Minju Kim’s continued success to Daniel Fletcher’s new role at Fiorucci, each contestant has carved out their own path in the fashion industry. While the show has not been renewed for a second season, its impact on these designers’ lives is undeniable. They continue to innovate, collaborate, and inspire, proving that “Next In Fashion” was just the beginning of their fashion journeys.
